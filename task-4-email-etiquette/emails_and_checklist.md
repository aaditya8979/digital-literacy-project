# Task 4: Email Etiquette & Communication

## Professional Email Samples

### Email 1: Assignment Deadline Extension Request

Subject: Request for Assignment Deadline Extension

Dear Professor [Name],

I am writing to request a brief extension on the [Assignment Name] due on [Original Date]. Due to [legitimate reason - illness, family emergency, technical issues], I have been unable to complete the assignment to the standard I strive for. I would greatly appreciate an extension of [number of days - typically 2-3 days] to submit quality work.

I have already completed [mention any progress] and am committed to submitting by [proposed new date]. Thank you for considering my request.

Respectfully,
[Your Name]
[Student ID]

### Email 2: Internship Interest Expression

Subject: Interest in Summer Internship Position

Dear [Hiring Manager Name],

I am writing to express my strong interest in the summer internship position at [Company Name]. With my background in [relevant field] and hands-on experience in [specific skills], I am enthusiastic about contributing to your team. I have attached my resume for your review and would welcome the opportunity to discuss how I can add value to your organization.

Thank you for your consideration.

Best regards,
[Your Name]

## Responsible Social Media Use: Do's and Don'ts

### Do's:
- Do maintain a professional online presence
- Do think before posting anything
- Do respect others' privacy and dignity
- Do verify information before sharing
- Do use privacy settings appropriately

### Don'ts:
- Don't post embarrassing or offensive content
- Don't share others' personal information without permission
- Don't engage in cyberbullying or harassment
- Don't post sensitive academic or financial information
- Don't respond impulsively to controversial topics

## "Reply-All" Mishap Reflection

Scenario: A student accidentally sends a personal complaint about a group project to the entire class mailing list instead of to the instructor.

What happened: The student's critical comment about group members' work ethic became visible to everyone, causing embarrassment and damaging team relationships.

How it could have been avoided: Before hitting send, the student should have verified the recipient list, used the 'to' field carefully instead of 'reply-all', and considered whether the message was appropriate for the intended audience. A simple review of the draft before sending would have prevented this incident.
